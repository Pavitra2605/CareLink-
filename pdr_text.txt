Project Requirement Document (PRD) Rural Healthcare Accessibility Platform Document Version: 1.0 Date: January 27, 2026 Project Status: Proposed 1. Executive Summary 1.1 Project Overview The Rural Healthcare Accessibility Platform is a comprehensive digital health solution designed to address critical healthcare gaps in rural and semi-urban regions of developing areas. The platform combines telemedicine, digital health records, AI-powered medical guidance, and medicine availability tracking to provide accessible, affordable, and quality healthcare to underserved populations. 1.2 Vision Statement To democratize healthcare access by empowering rural communities with technology-enabled medical services that overcome barriers of distance, infrastructure, and information availability. 1.3 Project Goals • Reduce unnecessary hospital visits by 40% through intelligent triage and telemedicine • Enable 90% of patients to maintain digital health records within the first year • Provide immediate medical guidance for common incidents within 2 minutes Connect patients with doctors within 15 minutes during operating hours Reduce medication unavailability issues by 60% through real-time tracking 2. Problem Statement 2.1 Current Challenges Healthcare Infrastructure Gaps Rural hospitals operate at less than 50% of sanctioned medical capacity • Severe shortage of specialists and general practitioners • Poor physical infrastructure and medical equipment Patients from multiple villages depend on single healthcare facilities Patient Accessibility Issues • Long travel distances (often 20-50 km) for basic consultations • Loss of daily income due to hospital visits High transportation costs Extended waiting times discouraging follow-up care Doctor or specialist unavailability Medical Information Fragmentation No structured medical history for patients • • • Paper-based prescriptions scattered or lost Doctors lack access to past conditions, allergies, or treatments Critical information unavailable during emergencies Repetitive diagnostic procedures Lack of Immediate Guidance Uncertainty during medical incidents (cuts, burns, injuries, sudden pain) Delays in treatment due to indecision Unnecessary emergency visits Unsafe self-treatment practices Increased anxiety and poor outcomes 2.2 Impact on Target Population • Daily-wage workers: Loss of income and financial burden Farmers: Disruption of agricultural activities during peak seasons Elderly individuals: Physical difficulty traveling long distances Women and children: Limited mobility and healthcare access Emergency cases: Delayed critical care leading to preventable complications 3.
